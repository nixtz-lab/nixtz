Module 2: Master Value Investment Strategy

With your foundation secure, it's time to learn the TMT core method: how to find great companies trading for less than they're worth.

Price is What You Pay, Value is What You Get

This quote from Warren Buffett is the heart of value investing. The stock market is full of noise. The price of a stock can swing wildly based on news, fear, or hype. Our job is to ignore the noise and find the *true* underlying value (the "intrinsic value") of a business. If we can buy a business for significantly less than it's worth, we have a "margin of safety."

The TMT Valuation Method

1. Find a Great Business

We don't buy "stocks"; we buy pieces of businesses. A great business has:

  • A "Moat": A durable competitive advantage (like a strong brand, patent, or network effect) that protects it from competitors.
  • Good Management: Honest and effective leaders who act in the best interest of shareholders.
  • Consistent Earnings: A long history of predictable, growing profits. We avoid speculative companies.

2. Calculate Its Intrinsic Value

This is where we use a financial model. The TMT model is a simplified "Discounted Cash Flow" (DCF) analysis. In simple terms, we:

  1. Project Future Earnings: Based on the company's past growth, we make conservative assumptions about how much its earnings will grow over the next 5-10 years.
  2. Apply a Multiple: We estimate what a reasonable "exit multiple" (like a P/E ratio) will be at the end of our projection.
  3. Discount Back to Today: We "discount" those future earnings back to today's dollars, using our desired annual rate of return (e.g., 10-15%). This gives us the Intrinsic Value.

3. Wait for the Right Price

Once we know a stock's intrinsic value (e.g., $150), we wait for the market to offer it to us at a discount (e.g., $100). This is our **Margin of Safety**. This patience is what separates successful investors from gamblers.
