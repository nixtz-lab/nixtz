Cookie Policy

Last Updated: October 20, 2024

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how Think Money Tree uses them, and your choices regarding their use.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small pieces of data stored on your device (computer or mobile device) when you browse a website. They are widely used to allow websites to function, or function more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies primarily to ensure the core functionality of our site and to remember your preferences, such as your decision to accept our cookie policy. We use the following types of cookies:

Essential Cookies (Required)

These cookies are strictly necessary to provide you with services available through our Website and to use some of its features, such as remembering your sign-in status or your security preferences.

  • Functionality Cookies: Used to remember choices you make (like your cookie consent decision) and provide a more personalized experience.
  • Security Cookies: Used for security purposes, such as to prevent fraudulent use of login credentials.

Performance and Analytics Cookies (Optional)

These cookies are used to collect information about traffic to our Website and how users use the Website. The information gathered may include the number of visitors, the pages they visit, and how long they spend on the site. We use this information to help operate our Website more efficiently and to compile statistical reports.

  • We currently use limited or no third-party analytics, but reserve the right to integrate tools like Google Analytics in the future to understand user behavior.

3. Your Choices Regarding Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your cookie preferences by:

  • Accepting via the Banner: By clicking "Accept" on the cookie banner on our landing page, you consent to the use of cookies described in this policy.
  • Browser Controls: You can set or amend your web browser controls to accept or refuse cookies. If you choose to reject cookies, you may still use our Website, though your access to some functionality and areas of our Website may be restricted.
  • Managing Local Storage: The cookie consent itself is stored in your browser's local storage. You can typically clear this in your browser settings if you wish to revoke consent (which will cause the banner to reappear).

4. Changes to the Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time in order to reflect changes to the cookies we use or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. Please revisit this page regularly to stay informed about our use of cookies.

5. Where to Find More Information

If you have any questions about our use of cookies, please contact us via the contact methods available on our main website.
