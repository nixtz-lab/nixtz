Crowdfunding & Private Credit

Go beyond the stock market. Become the bank or the developer by investing in private real estate deals.

Two Ways to Participate

Equity Crowdfunding

You buy a small slice of ownership in a specific building or a fund of buildings. You make money if the property appreciates in value or generates rental income.

  • Higher potential returns (10% - 20%+)
  • Long lock-up periods (5+ years)
  • You are an owner (Junior position)

Real Estate Debt

You act as the bank. You lend money to a developer to renovate or build a property. You get paid back with interest on a fixed schedule.

  • Consistent, fixed income (7% - 12%)
  • Shorter terms (6 - 24 months)
  • Senior position (Paid before owners)

Detailed Comparison

Feature Equity Investing Debt Investing
Primary Goal Maximum Total Return (Growth) Steady Cash Flow (Income)
Risk Profile Higher (Market fluctuations) Lower (Backed by property collateral)
Liquidity Very Low (Hard to sell) Low (Wait until loan matures)
Tax Treatment Capital Gains (Potential benefits) Ordinary Income (Taxed at regular rate)

The Liquidity Warning

Unlike stocks or REITs, you cannot click a "Sell" button to get your money back instantly. Money invested in private real estate is often locked up for years. Only invest funds you will not need for the medium-to-long term.
